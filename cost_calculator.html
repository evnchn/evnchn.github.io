⚙ Data Processing Pipeline

1. NDVI / Vegetation Index Computation — Compute NDVI, EVI, CMRI from multispectral bands to assess mangrove health. Pre-computed indices available via NASA HLS-VI (free). GEE or Python+Rasterio process on-demand at no cost.

3. Change Detection — Compare classified extent maps across time to quantify mangrove gain/loss. Global Mangrove Watch provides free annual maps (1996-2020). Custom detection via GEE at $0. — Springer Review 2023

Why drones for carbon credits?

LiDAR penetrates dense mangrove canopy to map ground elevation, producing a Canopy Height Model (CHM). Tree heights feed into allometric equations to estimate Above-Ground Biomass (AGB), converted to carbon stock (×0.47). Studies show LiDAR-derived mangrove height estimates achieve R²=0.84-0.97 — MDPI Sustainability 2025

Multispectral cameras capture NDVI and leaf area index, mapping vegetation health and density. This verifies that restoration is progressing and canopy cover is increasing over time.

Combined payloads (e.g. DJI Zenmuse L2: LiDAR + RGB camera, ~$13K) produce more accurate AGB estimates than either sensor alone. Mangrove AGB ranges from 9-128 Mg/ha depending on maturity — Frontiers in Marine Science 2019

Under Verra VCS VM0033, above-ground biomass is a required carbon pool. Drone surveys provide cost-effective MRV data for tree height and density compared to manual field inventory — Taylor & Francis 2024

⚙ Data Processing Pipeline

1. Carbon Stock Calculation — Convert lab results (OC%, bulk density) to Mg C/ha using CIFOR Kauffman & Donato formula. R or Python scripts (free). — Formula Guide

2. Geostatistical Interpolation — Kriging to extrapolate point samples across the project area. R gstat (free) or PyKrige (free). ArcGIS Geostatistical Analyst: ~$2,000-4,000/yr. — MDPI Forests 2022

3. Uncertainty Quantification — Verra VCS requires 90% CI; if half-width >10% of mean, conservativeness deduction applies. Monte Carlo / bootstrap via R/Python (free). — VM0033

4. Database Management — Store multi-year core data with spatial metadata. PostgreSQL + PostGIS (free) following CIFOR SWAMP schema. VM0033 requires 2+ year retention beyond crediting period.

5. MRV Reporting — Verra-compliant monitoring reports. PDD development: $30K-150K (shared across bond). VVB verification: $30K-50K per cycle (~every 5 yrs). Issuance fee: $0.20/VCU. — Verra Blue Carbon; TNC Blue Carbon Cost Tool

Why soil carbon sampling matters

Mangrove soils store 49-98% of total ecosystem carbon, primarily in the top 1 meter. Under Verra VCS VM0033, soil organic carbon is a required carbon pool that must be measured at baseline and monitored over the 30-year crediting period.

The standard protocol (Kauffman & Donato 2012) involves extracting soil cores at 5 depth increments, measuring bulk density and organic carbon content via combustion analysis. Carbon stock = Organic C% × bulk density × depth × 0.1 = Mg C/ha.

Conservation potential: $1,467-$28,099/ha over 40 years. Restoration potential: $1,669-$31,961/ha over 40 years — Jakovac et al., Ecological Economics 2020

2026-02-26 — IoT Sensor Density Fix

What went wrong: The IoT sensor density default was set to 1.5 nodes/ha, applied across the entire project site (1,000 ha). This produced 1,500 sensor nodes per project — a CapEx of ~$1.58M and OpEx of ~$603K/yr per site. Rolled up across 10 projects over a 10-year tenor, the total technology cost reached ~$78M, or 156% of the $50M bond principal. The density figure (1-3 nodes/ha) comes from FAO guidelines for within-pond sensor spacing, but was incorrectly applied as a site-wide density. In practice, only a small fraction of aquaculture ponds on a site are instrumented with sensors.

How the new calculation fixes it: The default density was lowered to 0.1 nodes/ha (site-wide average), reflecting that sensors are placed in representative ponds, not every hectare. This yields ~100 nodes per 1,000 ha site — a realistic deployment. With this fix, lifetime technology cost drops to ~$7.3M, or ~14.7% of the bond, which aligns with the team's expectation of technology costs being a manageable fraction of the bond proceeds.
